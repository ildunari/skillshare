# Preset: warm-human

Use for messages to humans where tone matters: apologies, feedback, support, sensitive updates, friendly announcements.

## Style

- Friendly, calm, and respectful.
- Natural contractions are fine (“we’ll”, “you’re”).
- Short paragraphs. Soft transitions.
- Keep it sincere; avoid cheesy optimism.

## Signature moves

- Acknowledge impact briefly, then move to what happens next.
- Use one concrete next step (date/time/action) instead of a vague “we’ll follow up.”
- End with a specific offer or question (“If you want, I can…”, “Does X work for you?”).

## Do

- Acknowledge feelings or impact if relevant.
- Be specific about next steps and ownership.
- Use simple language.

## Don’t

- Don’t over-comfort (“everything will be amazing”).
- Don’t get formal and robotic.
- Don’t slip into support-ticket templates (“we understand how frustrating this must be”) unless the source already uses that register.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Corporate empathy clichés and over-apologies.
- “This underscores…” style framing that sounds performative.
- Ending with a generic upbeat line instead of a real next step.
