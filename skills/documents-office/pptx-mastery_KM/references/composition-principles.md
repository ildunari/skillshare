# Composition principles

Use this guide to convert a chosen archetype into a visually balanced slide without improvising placements.

## Composition stack

Treat composition as a layered system:

1. Grid scaffold (margins, columns, gutters)
2. Primary focal anchor (headline, metric, hero image, or chart)
3. Secondary support blocks (cards, labels, evidence)
4. Tertiary cues (icons, dividers, annotations)

If layers 1 and 2 are weak, do not add layer 4.

## Focal hierarchy

Each slide should have one primary focal anchor.

| Pattern | Best archetypes | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Text-first anchor | A3, A5, A6, A10 | Headline remains the largest text role |
| Number-first anchor | A7 | Hero number dominates area and contrast |
| Visual-first anchor | A1, A4, A22 | Image or quote occupies dominant region |
| Data-first anchor | A17, A18, A19 | Chart/table carries main visual weight |

Quality check: if two regions compete equally for attention, demote one with lower contrast or smaller size.

## Alignment and rhythm

- Snap all major left edges to shared grid columns.
- Use consistent vertical rhythm increments (0.1 in or 0.125 in).
- Keep mirrored layouts truly mirrored (same padding and baseline alignment).
- Avoid fractional micro-offsets unless needed for optical correction.

## Balance patterns

Use one of three balance intents per slide:

| Intent | When to use | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Symmetric | comparisons, formal content | left/right mass is intentionally equal |
| Weighted | hero + support | one dominant region, one quiet region |
| Axial | timelines, processes | eye follows a clear directional axis |

Do not mix intents on a single slide.

## Whitespace budgeting

- Target whitespace ratio: `0.30-0.45`
- Minimum hard floor: `0.22`
- Section-divider and quote slides may go higher (`0.45-0.65`)

Whitespace is not empty area; it is separation that preserves hierarchy.

## Archetype composition cues

| Archetype family | Composition cue | Failure pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Hero (`A1`, `A4`, `A7`, `A22`) | One anchor, minimal secondary elements | Adding dense bullets under hero element |
| Grid (`A8`, `A9`, `A23`) | Equal card widths and consistent paddings | Mixed card heights with random gutters |
| Comparison (`A10`, `A11`, `A20`) | Mirrored scaffolding, contrasted content | Unequal columns causing perceived bias |
| Flow (`A12`, `A13`, `A14`, `A15`) | Clear directional path and node spacing | Connector crossings and cramped labels |
| Data (`A17`, `A18`, `A19`) | Chart/table area gets priority | Oversized decorative callouts |

## Fast composition QA checklist

1. Can the viewer identify the main point in 3 seconds?
2. Are there <= 3 dominant edge clusters?
3. Is the spacing rhythm consistent across sibling elements?
4. Does the slide keep its intended balance pattern?
5. Does the whitespace ratio remain within target?
