## Photorealism principles

Use the word `photorealistic` when realism is required. Pair it with believable light, material, lens/framing, and imperfections. Avoid overloading with camera specs unless the exact look matters; the model interprets specs loosely.

Good realism cues:

- “real camera”, “natural daylight”, “unposed”, “subtle film grain”;
- “visible pores”, “fabric weave”, “contact shadows”, “slight clutter”;
- “not glossy”, “not cinematic”, “not heavily retouched” when authenticity matters.

## Documentary realism

Best for grounded human scenes, events, travel, streets, work, and everyday life.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic documentary photograph of [subject/action] in [setting].
Framing: [wide/medium/close], eye-level, natural composition.
Lighting: real available light, [weather/time], believable shadows.
Details: [specific clothing, materials, environment].
Mood: honest, unposed, observational.
Avoid cinematic grading, glamour retouching, exaggerated poses, and artificial lighting.
```

## Candid flash snapshot

Best for party photos, nightlife, social scenes, nostalgic compact-camera looks.

Template:

```text
Create a candid flash photograph of [people/scene] at [setting].
Look: direct on-camera flash, slight motion blur, casual framing, imperfect composition, real skin texture, high-energy moment.
Background: [specific environmental details].
Avoid polished studio lighting, fashion-pose perfection, and over-smoothing.
```

## Smartphone photo realism

Best for user-generated content, marketplace listings, casual travel, and believable everyday images.

Template:

```text
Create a realistic smartphone photo of [subject] taken by an ordinary person.
Framing: slightly imperfect, natural perspective, no staged lighting.
Lighting: [indoor/window/overcast/golden hour].
Details: believable background clutter, normal color balance, real-world texture.
Avoid professional studio look, cinematic color grading, and perfect symmetry.
```

## Editorial portrait

Best for profiles, magazine portraits, creator headshots, and polished character images.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic editorial portrait of [subject] in [setting].
Composition: [half body/headshot/environmental], strong but natural pose, direct or thoughtful gaze.
Lighting: soft directional light, subtle background separation.
Style: premium magazine portrait, real skin texture, restrained retouching, tasteful wardrobe.
Avoid plastic skin, fake bokeh, exaggerated beauty lighting, and over-styled props.
```

## Fashion editorial

Best for luxury campaigns, lookbooks, streetwear, product-on-person visuals.

Template:

```text
Create a high-fashion editorial photograph for [brand/concept].
Subject: [model/look/garments], pose [pose], setting [setting].
Art direction: [minimal luxury / gritty street / avant-garde], cohesive palette, premium styling.
Lighting: [studio softbox / natural urban / dramatic editorial].
Constraints: original non-infringing wardrobe, no real brand logos, no watermark, no extra text unless specified.
```

## Food photography

Best for restaurants, cookbooks, ads, recipe images, and menu visuals.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic food photograph of [dish].
Composition: [overhead/three-quarter/macro], plate/bowl details [details].
Lighting: soft window light, natural shadows, appetizing texture.
Styling: [rustic/minimal/editorial], realistic crumbs/steam/sauce sheen.
Avoid plastic-looking food, impossible garnish, messy unreadable plating, and fake logos.
```

## Product photography

Best for ecommerce, packaging, catalog, hero renders, and ads.

Template:

```text
Create a premium product photograph of [product] on [background].
Composition: centered hero product with generous negative space.
Lighting: soft studio lighting, realistic contact shadow, crisp edges.
Material detail: [glass/metal/plastic/paper/fabric] texture visible.
Constraints: preserve label text if provided, no extra logos, no watermark, no floating artifacts.
```

## Architecture and interiors

Best for real estate, concept interiors, furniture swaps, architectural mood boards.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic interior photograph of [room/type].
Architecture: [style], materials [materials], furniture [furniture].
Lighting: natural window light, realistic shadows, balanced exposure.
Camera: wide but natural perspective, vertical lines straight, no fisheye distortion.
Mood: lived-in but clean.
Avoid impossible room geometry, warped furniture, fake reflections, and over-staging.
```

## Macro and material close-up

Best for texture studies, product details, scientific visual texture, cosmetics, jewelry.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic macro photograph of [subject/material].
Focus: [surface detail], shallow depth of field, crisp microtexture.
Lighting: soft directional highlights, realistic shadows.
Material behavior: [translucent/metallic/fibrous/wet/powdery].
Avoid abstract blur, fake pattern repetition, and unnatural scale.
```

## Cinematic film still

Best for storytelling, concept art, mood frames, storyboards, and fictional scenes.

Template:

```text
Create a cinematic film still of [scene/action].
Composition: [wide/medium/close], strong blocking, atmospheric depth.
Lighting: [low-key/neon dusk/soft overcast], controlled color palette.
Production design: [setting details], believable props, lived-in world.
Mood: [tone].
Avoid poster-like composition, over-sharpening, fake lens flare, and text overlays.
```

## Surreal realism

Best for uncanny editorial images, dreamlike portraits, symbolic scenes, and visual metaphors.

Template:

```text
Create a photorealistic surreal scene of [impossible idea] in [realistic setting].
Make the impossible element physically integrated with believable lighting, shadows, and material texture.
Mood: [deadpan/quiet/eerie/whimsical].
Composition: restrained, not chaotic; one clear focal point.
Avoid fantasy illustration style, excessive symbols, and cluttered dream logic.
```
